the problem

  • procurement data comes from all kinds of different sources, each with their own set of unique challenge:s
    • dated legacy systems vs. state of the art data management
    • cleanliness of manual entries
    • massive storage and memory demands

  • sources are often highly connected
    • critical for comprehensive analysis, but complex

  • this is where our work at Rotunda Solutions comes in!

data collection

    • are any NAICS codes very specific to a single vendor/location?

conflict of interest detection

  • given a company (e.g. "Rotunda Solutions"), how can we identify as many currently active and connected programs that the company was a prime/subcontractor on?

  • example: Sam was promoted to Defense Secretary. How can he get ahead of any potential accusations of conflicts of interest with Rotunda?
    • imagine if the 'QWERTY' weapons program awarded tons of work to Rotunda...
    • what if a subsidary 'Adnutor Solutions' was associated with the program?
